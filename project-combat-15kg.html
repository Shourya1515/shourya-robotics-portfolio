15 KG Combat Robot – 1st Place (Anna University Chennai)

Mid-weight combat robot optimized for pushing power, low profile, and reliability. This robot won 1st place and a ₹40,000 cash prize at the National Tech Fest conducted by Anna University Chennai.

Gallery

Project Overview

The 15 KG robot focuses on controlled aggression rather than pure destructive power. A rigid, low-center-of-gravity chassis and front wedge geometry allow the robot to get under opponents, disrupt their stability, and control the fight. High-torque brushless motors provide strong pushing force and acceleration.

Technical Breakdown

  • CNC-machined aluminum frame with reinforced corner blocks.
  • Four-wheel drive using high-torque BLDC motors and gearboxes.
  • Low ground-clearance front wedge for lifting and destabilizing other bots.
  • LiPo power system with high current ESCs and safety isolation.
  • Modular electronics bay for fast swapping of ESCs / receivers.
  • Designed around weight balancing for responsive driving and traction.

Outcome

Across multiple matches, the robot showed outstanding durability and control, dominating pushing battles and winning on aggression and ring control. This project sharpened my skills in combat-oriented mechanical design, drivetrain tuning, and competition strategy.
