Omni-Directional Robot

Kinematics · Holonomic Drive · Motion Control

A holonomic mobile platform capable of smooth 360° motion, built around a full kinematic model and Jacobian-based inverse kinematics for independent control of translational and rotational velocities.

Concept

The omni-directional robot is designed to move freely in any planar direction without reorienting its chassis. By using omni wheels and a carefully derived kinematic model, the platform can track desired linear and angular velocity commands simultaneously.

Kinematics

  • Derived forward and inverse kinematics for a 3/4-wheel omni base.
  • Jacobian formulation mapping wheel speeds to chassis twist.
  • Decoupled control of vx, vy, ω.

Control & Simulation

  • Implemented velocity controllers for individual wheel motors.
  • Tested path-following behavior for circular and L-shaped paths.
  • Validated kinematics through simulation before physical build.

Outcome

This project builds the mathematical and control foundation for future omni-drive robots, where precise lateral motion and reorientation are critical—such as warehouse logistics or inspection platforms.
