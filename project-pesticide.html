Pesticide Spraying Robot

A low-cost mobile robot designed to help farmers reduce direct exposure to harmful pesticides during field spraying operations. The robot is manually controlled using a remote controller and incorporates a pumping mechanism with a horizontal and vertical spray pipe system.

Gallery

Project Overview

The robot is built on a sturdy 4-wheel platform powered by four independent motors, allowing it to travel across typical farm surfaces. A pesticide pump is mounted on the chassis and connected to a custom-designed spraying mechanism made from lightweight PVC pipes. The mechanism supports both horizontal and vertical spraying to cover a wide crop area.

The system is controlled remotely, enabling farmers to operate the sprayer from a safe distance, significantly reducing their direct contact with chemicals.

Technical Breakdown

  • Drive System: 4-wheel, 4-motor platform for stable movement on uneven fields.
  • Spray Mechanism: Adjustable PVC pipe system for horizontal and vertical spraying.
  • Pumping Unit: Electric pesticide pump mounted on the body.
  • Control: Manual operation via remote controller for safe distancing.
  • Purpose: Reduce human exposure to chemicals while improving spray consistency.

Outcome

This project demonstrates how robotics can directly assist agriculture by improving worker safety and efficiency. The robot successfully delivered uniform spray coverage while removing farmers from hazardous chemicals, showcasing a practical application of field robotics.
