About Me

I’m a robotics engineer who enjoys taking robots from CAD and simulation all the way to working systems. I work heavily with ROS / ROS2, SLAM, Nav2, LiDAR, sensor fusion, and real-time perception to build platforms that can map, localize, and navigate reliably in real environments.

My experience spans autonomous 4-wheel robots, omni-directional kinematics, combat robotics, agricultural robots, and analytical reporting pipelines. I like combining hands-on building with clean documentation, visualizations, and data-driven insight so other people can understand and repeat what I’ve built.

What I Do

I build complete robotic systems—from mechanical design and simulation to autonomous navigation and real-time perception. My work spans ROS2 development, SLAM pipelines, LiDAR and camera integration, Nav2 navigation, URDF/Xacro modeling, kinematics, and multi-sensor fusion. I have hands-on experience developing autonomous 4-wheel robots, omni-directional platforms, and competition-grade combat robots, along with optimizing robot hardware for real-world environments.

How I Work

I take an engineering-first, experiment-driven approach: prototype fast, validate through simulation, refine through testing, and optimize for reliability. I enjoy solving problems across the full stack—mechanical, electrical, and software—and I document each stage with clarity. Whether tuning SLAM, analyzing control behavior, or reinforcing a robot chassis for impact loads, I focus on building robust systems that perform consistently under pressure.

What I’m Seeking

I’m looking for robotics, autonomy, or intelligent systems co-op roles where I can contribute to real-world robot development—whether in perception, navigation, controls, or system integration. I want to work on teams pushing the boundaries of mobile robotics, field robotics, and autonomous platforms, while continuing to grow in ROS2, SLAM, motion planning, sensor fusion, and robot simulation.
