About Me

I’m a robotics engineer who loves taking robots from CAD to code. I work across the full stack – modelling in Fusion 360, bringing up robots in ROS2, and tuning SLAM & Nav2 so they can reliably move through the world. Most of my projects combine LiDAR, IMU, cameras, and custom controllers with clean software structure and documentation.

I’ve built autonomous 4-wheel robots, omni-directional platforms, combat robots, agricultural robots, and sensor-fusion pipelines. I enjoy debugging at the terminal as much as I enjoy tightening the last bolt on a chassis, and I care a lot about repeatable experiments, good plots, and readable code.

What I Do

Design, simulate, and deploy robots end-to-end: CAD → Gazebo / RViz → ROS2 bring-up → SLAM, localization, and autonomous navigation with real-time visualization and logging.

How I Work

Start simple, iterate fast, and validate with experiments. I like version-controlled configs, structured launch files, and clear plots that show whether a change actually helped.

What I’m Seeking

Co-op / internship roles where I can work on autonomy, perception, and robot platforms – especially teams using ROS2, SLAM, Nav2, or hardware-in-the-loop testing.
